Cultural Blog: Issues in Tech

March 22, 2015

In a world where personal information is so easily obtained, the question facing so many people is:

How do I keep what I don’t want known, private?

On this Friday’s Shark Tank, a young entrepreneur brought out an idea where people would have an easy time making sure their personal information didn’t come up in Google searches.

Later that evening, my husband and I were talking over dinner about a woman in his office who uses the dating app Tinder. After she looked at her potential dates for the evening, she started doing research about them. In a few minutes, just from checking their facebook and LinkedIn profiles, she knew where they worked, what they liked, and was able to look through past photos and experiences without any barriers.

It got me thinking that in a world where everything is so available, how can individuals truly have any privacy at all? Major corporations invest millions of dollars each year protecting information that is vital to their operations, but what about the average person who has little to no disposable income to invest in security?

Is all of their information up for grabs because they don’t have the means to build a proper firewall around it?

I believe that in the future, people will not be so free to share information and that, soon, social media platforms will start building private communities where people will only show information to those they wish to see it.

Protecting information will soon be the number one-ask of any consumer that puts their lives online.
