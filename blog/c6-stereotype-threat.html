Cultural Blog: Stereotype Threat

April 5, 2015

Up until now, I haven’t really thought about how other people’s stereo typing of me affects my actions. I’m originally from Mongolia, but, despite that fact, I am usually thrown in with all other Asians. I am, in many people’s eyes, Asian.

So, when people meet me, they think I’m passive, quiet and submissive. I often get lines thrown my way like “Oh, you must be very sweet” or “I have a friend who is from Japan and she is just a darling.” In my mind, I am wondering if I should say “Oh, I know someone from Wisconsin and they like to eat potatoes!”

However, now I am wondering if how other people see me actually changes the way I act. Do I say “sorry” too often? Am I less likely to speak my mind in a group setting because I already know what people are thinking of me?

Before I decided to dedicate my days and nights to coding at Dev Bootcamp, I was an interior designer. I spent hours with clients showcasing my designs to them, but there were times when they pushed back and made choices that I believed ruined the plan I had laid out for them. Though I protested, I wonder now if Stereotype Threat made me more submissive than I should have been.

After all, in my head, I knew what was right, but for some reason, I didn’t fully fight for what I believed to be correct. However, now that I am aware of the condition, I’ll make sure that I will act exactly how I feel is right rather than how other’s believe I should act. It’s very interesting to think that the energy of someone else’s opinion can influence you so firmly.

To other people out there who might come under a Stereotype Threat, I would urge them to listen to their guts in regards to what they should be saying or doing. Our society places people into these neat little boxes where you are expected to stay. If you come out of that stereotype, people are going to look at you strange and want you to act how they feel you should act.

There is a great danger of doing so even if you are not fully aware you are doing it. For this reason, I will be mindful of my actions and urge others to be the same. After all, if you can control what you do, you’ll break free of the one person who could be holding you back:

Yourself.
