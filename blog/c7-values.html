Cultural Blog: Values

April 12, 2015

Last week, after going over the Stereotype Threat in my head, I realized just how influenced I was by the thoughts of people around me. It really stuck with me, and had me examine how I think about myself. I came to realize that much of what I thought about myself was actually put there by other’s people’s beliefs.

Was this how I always was, or did it start when I came to America?

I started to ask the question: Had my values - what I believed to be true about myself, vanished and been replaced by false judgements of others. To retrieve a bit of myself and combat the effects of Stereotype Threat, I went back to my basic values in an attempt to build a foundation to battle against the thoughts of others.

For me, my most essential value was self-confidence and a belief that I was strong enough to accomplish whatever I set my mind to. I have always been a very confident person, but since moving to America more than 10 years ago, I may have let other people’s perception of me erode some of that. I often feel that if you don’t speak English perfectly here, Americans judge you as being not “up to their standards.” They get frustrated if you struggle to find a word or express yourself perfectly. I have found this to be true in both work and social settings.

For example, if I am in a store asking the clerk about a product, they appear to treat me as less important than an native speaker. I may have let their perceptions about intelligence influence how I thought about my own ability.

However, if I stop to think about it, I not only speak my native language, but I have learned English fluently. Can they say the same? What would happen if they went to another country and were forced not only to learn the language, but to survive financially? I am proud that I have done that here, so my core value of confidence can help to battle Stereotype Threat in that regard.

I would urge all other students who are not native English speakers to take heart and have confidence that they are in a different country yet have still managed to learn the language and flourish. This affirmation will help you battle prejudice and other phobias that may be thrown your way.
